# SOP 07 — Seller Journey Management
**Owner:** Agent | **Platform:** `/portal/crm` | **Avg time on market 2026:** 210 days

## Journey Overview

```
MANDATE → LIST → MARKET → VISITS → OFFER → NEGOTIATION → CPCV → ESCRITURA → HANDOVER
  D0        D7     D7+      D14+     D60+      D90+         D120+    D150–210+    D210+
```

The seller journey is built on trust, transparency, and consistent communication. Sellers who feel informed are less likely to reduce price prematurely or pull the mandate.

---

## Stage 1 — MANDATE
**Entry:** Seller interest confirmed | **Exit:** Mandate signed, property fully documented

### Actions (D0–7)
1. Deliver valuation and pitch (SOP 05 Phase 2–3)
2. Collect and verify all legal documents (SOP 09 Pre-CPCV seller checklist)
3. Sign mandate agreement
4. Input property into CRM: status = `mandate_signed`
5. Schedule photography and staging session
6. Issue welcome email to seller: expected timeline, communication plan

### Seller Onboarding Communication
Within 24h of mandate signing:
- WhatsApp: confirm next steps and photography date
- Email: send "Your Property Marketing Plan" (template: `seller-onboarding`)

### Seller Onboarding Email Contents
- Photography date and preparation tips
- Portal listing timeline (live within 7 days of photography)
- External portals where property will appear
- Marketing channels: social media, international buyer network, Sofia AI
- Reporting cadence: weekly vendor report every Friday
- Primary contact: agent name, direct phone, WhatsApp

## Stage 3 — MARKET
**Entry:** Listing live | **Exit:** First qualified visit scheduled

### Ongoing Marketing Activities
- Weekly: refresh photos or featured placement on portals
- Weekly: share to social media (rotation: photo, video, market stat)
- Monthly: international buyer newsletter feature (if >€500K)
- On demand: WhatsApp campaign to matched buyer leads

### Market Activity Monitoring
Track weekly:
- Portal views (Idealista, Imovirtual, AG portal)
- Inquiry volume (qualified vs. unqualified)
- Match engine hits (how many buyer profiles match)
- Portal position vs. competition

### Vendor Report — Weekly (Every Friday)
**Owner:** Assigned agent | **Format:** WhatsApp summary + email detail
**SLA:** Delivered every Friday by 18h, regardless of activity level

**Vendor Report Contents:**
```
AG Vendor Report — [Property Ref] — Week [W]
Property: [Address]
Date: [DD/MM/YYYY]

ACTIVITY THIS WEEK:
• Portal views: [X] (AG) + [Y] (Idealista) + [Z] (Imovirtual)
• Inquiries received: [X] (qualified: Y)
• Visits completed: [X]
• Visits scheduled: [X next week]

MARKET UPDATE:
• New competing listings in zone: [X]
• Average price movement: [+/-X%]
• Comparable sold this week: [ref if available]

FEEDBACK FROM VISITS:
• [Summarize key feedback — price, condition, location comments]

OUTLOOK:
• Next steps: [what agent is doing next week]

```

**Rule:** Even if there is zero activity, send the report. Silence destroys seller trust.

## Stage 4 — VISITS
**Entry:** Visit request received | **Exit:** Offer received or pipeline updated

### Visit Preparation
- Notify seller of visit: minimum 24h notice (or agreed upon mandate terms)
- Confirm property clean and staged
- Check for competing offers or interest from other buyers
- Prepare property highlights sheet for visiting buyer's agent

### Visit Feedback Protocol
Within 4h of every visit:
1. Record visitor details in CRM (agent, buyer, motivation level)
2. Record detailed feedback (verbally from buyer agent or own observation)
3. Forward feedback to seller same day
4. Categorize feedback:
   - Price objection → log for pricing review
   - Condition objection → advise seller on specific improvements
   - Location objection → disqualified (cannot change)
   - "Just looking" → likely unqualified, do not prioritize

### Managing Multiple Visits
If multiple showings scheduled in same week:
- Coordinate times to avoid overlap
- Brief each buyer agent separately on property highlights
- After multiple visits with no offers: call seller, present aggregated feedback

## Stage 5 — OFFER RECEIVED
**Entry:** Written or WhatsApp offer from buyer | **Exit:** Accepted, countered, or rejected

### Offer Presentation Protocol
1. Receive offer in writing (email minimum, even if WhatsApp summary)
2. Verify buyer credentials (proof of funds or mortgage approval)
3. Present to seller within 24h (in person or video call for significant offers)
4. Never present an offer without context: "This is [% of] asking, comparable to [recent sale]"
5. Advise, don't decide: the seller decides, the agent informs

### Offer Analysis Framework
Present to seller:
- Offer price vs. asking price (% spread)
- Net proceeds after transaction costs (IMT, IS, notário, certificate updates)
- Buyer profile (cash vs. mortgage, timeline, location of buyer)
- Market context (competing offers? Days on market?)
- Our recommendation (accept / counter / reject)

### Counter-Offer Guidance
If seller wants to counter:
- Identify acceptable range before counter is sent
- Counter within 24h of receiving offer
- Never let a counter-offer sit >48h without acknowledgment
- Apply negotiation framework (SOP 08)

## Stage 6 — DAYS ON MARKET THRESHOLDS

### Price Reduction Protocol

| Days on Market | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 30 days | No qualified visit | Review portal positioning and marketing |
| 45 days | No offer | Present market feedback, recommend 3–5% price reduction |
| 60 days | No offer | Mandatory pricing review meeting with seller |
| 90 days | No offer | Full reassessment: AVM refresh + competition analysis |
| 120 days | No offer | Price correction or mandate review |
| 180 days | No offer | Suspend listing, full strategy reset |

### Price Reduction Meeting (60 days)
Agenda:
1. Present all visit feedback aggregated
2. Show current competition (similar properties at what price, days on market)
3. Present AVM refreshed
4. Show "days on market vs. price" curve for zone
5. Propose price adjustment with scenarios: expected outcome at each price point
6. Agree on new price in writing (amendment to mandate)

### Price Reduction Communication
After seller agrees to price reduction:
- Update portals immediately
- Send internal notification to all agents with buyer matches
- Re-run match engine with new price
- WhatsApp to top 3 matched buyers: "Price adjustment on [ref] — now at €X"

## Stage 7 — OPEN HOUSE (for select properties)

### When to Run an Open House
- Suitable for: properties in popular residential zones, €200K–€1.5M range
- Not suitable for: ultra-high-security properties, very rural, or where seller doesn't consent

### Open House Preparation (1 week prior)
- [ ] Seller consent and date confirmed
- [ ] Portal announcement with "Open House — [Date]" tag
- [ ] Social media posts (3 days before, day before, day of)
- [ ] Email to matched buyer leads in CRM
- [ ] Whatsapp to local buyer agents network
- [ ] Printed brochures (minimum 20)
- [ ] Staging refreshed (deep clean, flowers, natural light)
- [ ] Agent arrives 30 min early

### Open House Execution
- Duration: 2 hours (e.g., 11h–13h Saturday)
- Register all visitors: name + phone + email
- Brief qualification of all visitors (not just sightseers)
- Follow up with every registered visitor within 24h

### Open House Follow-Up
- All visitors entered into CRM
- Qualified visitors: personal call within 24h
- Seller briefed on same day: traffic, feedback, next steps
