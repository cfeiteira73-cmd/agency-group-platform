## 1. Where SH-ROS Wins on Product

**1.1 Match Engine with Economic Calibration**
SH-ROS scores buyer-property matches against Portugal 2026 market actuals — €3.076/m² median, €5,000/m² Lisbon luxury, €4,713/m² Cascais, 210-day avg time-to-close. No competitor calibrates match scoring to real market economics in this geography. Follow Up Boss scores on behavioral signals (email opens, clicks). SH-ROS scores on economic fit between buyer capacity and property yield probability.

**1.2 Self-Healing Workflow Architecture**
Workflows don't just execute — they detect stall states, diagnose root cause, and self-correct. A deal that goes dark at day 45 triggers backpressure analysis, identifies the bottleneck (buyer financing, objection category, agent inaction), and routes the corrective action automatically. No human has to notice the deal is stalling. This is operationally irreplaceable once embedded.

**1.3 Distributed Event Bus with Replay**
Every revenue event is durable, replayable, and partition-tolerant. If a worker fails mid-deal-stage, state is not lost. For institutional agencies running €10M+ deal pipelines, data integrity is non-negotiable. No CRM competitor at sub-€10K/month delivers this infrastructure guarantee.

**1.4 AI Learning with Validator Gate**
The learning loop is not a black box. A validation gate requires demonstrable improvement (measured in closed deal rate, attribution accuracy) before any model update propagates to production. This explainability is required for enterprise adoption in EU-regulated environments and is absent in every competitor's AI offering.

**1.5 Revenue Attribution Engine**
SH-ROS closes the attribution loop: which touchpoint, which workflow intervention, which match recommendation contributed to which closed deal. This is not CRM activity logging. This is causal revenue attribution — the capability a CRO needs to justify budget, optimize team, and defend retention.

## 3. Where SH-ROS Wins on Architecture

**AI-Native vs Bolt-On AI:**
Salesforce added Einstein. HubSpot added Breeze. These are AI features added to CRM architecture — they cannot access the economic context needed for real estate decision-making because the data model was never built for it. SH-ROS was designed from first principles around AI inference at every decision point. The architecture is not retrofitted.

**Distributed vs Monolithic:**
Circuit breakers, backpressure, tenancy isolation, and FNV-1a partitioning are not features — they are the foundation. A monolithic CRM that goes down during peak season (summer Algarve, spring Lisbon) is a revenue event, not a support ticket. Distributed architecture eliminates single points of failure at the infrastructure layer.

**Feedback Loop vs Static Rules:**
Every automation competitor operates on if/then logic authored at setup. SH-ROS operates on feedback loops that update based on observed outcomes. The system that ran month 1 is materially inferior to the system in month 12 — not because it was updated by a consultant, but because it learned from €X in closed deals.
